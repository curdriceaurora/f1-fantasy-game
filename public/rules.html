You have £50m to spend on 3 drivers + 3 teams. No restrictions on combinations — you can pick 2 drivers from the same team, or even the team itself. Points come from qualifying performance, race results, your home circuit bonus, and your end-of-season predictions.

Each driver scores points based on their qualifying position relative to their assigned capability rank:

Key insight: "No Hoper" and "Outsider" drivers earn positive points for qualifying anywhere in the top half of the grid — making cheap, fast-qualifying drivers like Gasly, Lindblad, and Colapinto extremely valuable picks.

§2.2 — Race Points

Standard F1 finishing points: 1st 25 | 2nd 18 | 3rd 15 | 4th 12 | 5th 10 | 6th 8 | 7th 6 | 8th 4 | 9th 2 | 10th 1

Position change bonus: +2 pts per place gained, –2 pts per place lost vs. grid start position.

Fastest Lap: +2 pts. Sprint races: FIA sprint scoring (8→1 pts for top 8).

Team scoring: Sum of both drivers' points, weighted as (3 × Lead Driver + 2 × Second Driver) ÷ 5, rounded up.

§2.3 — Home Circuit Bonus

Pick one race circuit at the start. Your entire score for that race is doubled (driver + team). If the result is negative, it's reset to 0. If your circuit is cancelled, you may pick another.

§2.4 — Penalties

Grid penalties = minus points equal to places penalised (capped at 10). Time penalties at race end = minus seconds penalised (capped at 10).

§2.5 — Fines

FIA monetary fines: –1 pt per €2,000 (or part thereof), capped at 100 pts. Suspended fines = no penalty.

§2.6 — Investment Bonus

After Race 11 (Britain), you may replace one driver or one team. Costs 20 points deducted from your current total. Must stay within £50m. Must be submitted before Race 12 (Belgium). If a driver drops out permanently, free replacement at same cost — no penalty.

❓ FAQ

When are race scores published?
Standings are intended to be updated on Monday afternoon after each race weekend, once steward decisions, reclassifications, and applicable FIA fines are already reflected in the official records. There is no live scoring during the race weekend.
Which results count if something changes after the chequered flag?
The league uses the final official result state used for the Monday scoring run. That means late penalties, revised classifications, and applicable fine deductions are intended to count if they are official by the time Martin publishes that round.
How are FIA fines applied?
Per §2.5, FIA monetary fines score at –1 point per €2,000 or part thereof, capped at 100 points. Suspended fines do not count. If a fine applies to a driver or team in the rules, it is reflected only as the points deduction in the weekly score breakdown.
How does the Home Circuit bonus work?
Pick one race from the 2026 calendar at the start of the season. At that race, your driver subtotal + team subtotal is doubled. If the doubled total would be negative, it is reset to 0. Investment bonus is then added after that.
How does the Investment Bonus work?
Any unspent budget earns floor(unspent ÷ 2) points per race.
Example: Spend £44m → £6m unspent → +3 pts/race. Example: Spend £49m → £1m unspent → +0 pts/race.
Odd millions are ignored. The bonus applies every race, and it is shown in the dashboard as a separate points line.
What does "position change" mean in race scoring?
Position change is measured from the official starting grid position to the final classified race finish. Each place gained is worth +2; each place lost is worth –2.
How is a constructor score worked out?
Per §2.2, a constructor uses both of its real drivers’ scored totals for that weekend, weighted as (3 × Lead Driver + 2 × Second Driver) ÷ 5, rounded up. The higher-scoring driver is treated as the lead driver for that calculation.
Can I make a mid-season change?
Yes. After Race 11 (Britain), you may replace one driver or one team. That costs 20 points from your score and must be submitted before Race 12 (Belgium). If a driver drops out for the rest of the season, the replacement is free at the same cost.
What happens if my chosen Home Circuit race is cancelled?
If your chosen home-circuit round is cancelled, Martin can allow a replacement selection later in the season. If the race runs as scheduled, your original home circuit stays locked.
Do sprint weekends score differently?
Yes. Sprint rounds add FIA sprint scoring for the top 8 on top of the normal qualifying and grand prix scoring for that weekend.
What does "Total Classified" mean?
This is your pre-season prediction for the total number of classified drivers across all 24 races. With 22 drivers over 24 races, the maximum is 528. It is an end-of-season prediction item, not a weekly race score.
