📋 Rules

⚡ QUICK START — NEW HERE? READ THIS FIRST

  1. Pick 3 drivers and 3 teams from the table below
  2. Make sure your total cost ≤ £50m (use the calculator!)
  3. Pick a Home Circuit from the race calendar for a double-points bonus
  4. Fill in the 5 predictions for end-of-season bonus points
  5. Email Martin by Thursday 5th March 2026 with your £15 entry fee

You have £50m to spend on 3 drivers + 3 teams. No restrictions on combinations — you can pick 2 drivers from the same team, or even the team itself. Points come from qualifying performance, race results, your home circuit bonus, and your end-of-season predictions.

§2.2 — Race Points

Standard F1 finishing points: 1st 25 | 2nd 18 | 3rd 15 | 4th 12 | 5th 10 | 6th 8 | 7th 6 | 8th 4 | 9th 2 | 10th 1

Position change bonus: +2 pts per place gained, –2 pts per place lost vs. grid start position.

Fastest Lap: +2 pts. Sprint races: FIA sprint scoring (8→1 pts for top 8).

Team scoring: Sum of both drivers' points, weighted as (3 × Lead Driver + 2 × Second Driver) ÷ 5, rounded up.

§2.3 — Home Circuit Bonus

Pick one race circuit at the start. Your entire score for that race is doubled (driver + team). If the result is negative, it's reset to 0. If your circuit is cancelled, you may pick another.

§2.4 — Penalties

Grid penalties = minus points equal to places penalised (capped at 10). Time penalties at race end = minus seconds penalised (capped at 10).

§2.5 — Fines

FIA monetary fines: –1 pt per €2,000 (or part thereof), capped at 100 pts. Suspended fines = no penalty.

§2.6 — Investment Bonus

❓ FAQ

What does "Total Classified" mean?
A driver is "classified" if they were still running at the checkered flag, per the FIA official results. You predict the total count across all 24 races. With 22 drivers and 24 races, the maximum is 528 (if everyone finishes every race). Last year with 20 drivers the total was 407. Expect somewhere in the 420–460 range for 2026.
Can I pick two drivers from the same team?
Yes! There are no restrictions on combinations. You can even pick a team and both its drivers in the same entry — as long as the total cost stays within £50m.
How does the Home Circuit bonus work?
Pick any circuit from the 2026 calendar. At that race, your entire score is doubled — drivers and teams combined, including sprint points if applicable. If the doubled result is negative, it's reset to zero. If your circuit is cancelled, you may pick an alternative later in the season.
How does the Investment Bonus work exactly?
Any unspent budget earns you floor(unspent ÷ 2) points per race, every race.
Example: Spend £44m → £6m unspent → floor(6÷2) = 3 pts/race × 24 races = +72 pts season bonus Example: Spend £49m → £1m unspent → floor(1÷2) = 0 pts (odd millions are ignored!)
The maximum investment is £20m (cheapest possible team = 3×£5 drivers + 3×£5 teams = £30m), giving +10 pts/race, +240 pts over the season.
Can I change my entry before the deadline?
Yes — you can update your selection any time before 5th March 2026, 4pm, subject to your new combination not already being taken by someone else. Martin will let you know if there's a clash.
Can I change my team during the season?
After Race 11 (Britain), you may swap one driver or one team. This costs you 20 points deducted from your current total and must be submitted before Race 12 (Belgium). The replacement must keep your total within £50m (usually the incoming driver/team costs the same as the outgoing one).

If a driver drops out for the rest of the season, you get a free replacement at the same cost — no points penalty.
Why do No Hoper drivers score the most qualifying points?
The scoring matrix rewards outperforming expectations. A "No Hoper" driver who qualifies 6th–10th scores +9 points, while a "Champion" in the same position scores –4 points. This makes cheap, scrappy qualifiers like Gasly, Colapinto, and Lindblad potentially very lucrative picks — especially if they regularly punch above their weight.
How are prizes distributed?
£4 per entrant goes to charity. The rest is all prize money:
  • £3 for highest score in each individual race (×24 = £72 total)
  • £5 to last place at season end
  • £5 each to highest and lowest individual race score over the season
  • Remaining pot split among top finishers (number depends on total entries — roughly top 10 with ~25 entrants)
All payouts are higher than the £15 entry fee.
